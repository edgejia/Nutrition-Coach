# v3.4.1 Postmortem — Terminated

**Status:** terminated, unshipped, and not eligible for Phase 114–118 continuation.

The original v3.4.1 Public Runtime & Timed Demo Proof plan was stopped after its
read-only Phase 114 preflight and the bounded Phase 115-04 release-check
diagnostic. Phase 115 did not produce a releasable runtime proof; Phases 116–118
were not executed. Their archived planning files remain historical evidence and
are not an execution queue.

## What was retained

- Source-readiness intent remains in the normal PR policy and `Release Check`.
- Runtime safety remains behind explicit recovery, migration, build/start, and
  exact provenance checks.
- Public validation remains a separate named-Tunnel and same-origin browser
  outcome after the runtime gate.
- The five-minute timed demo is retired from v3.4.1 and is not a fourth gate.

## Lean deployment gates

1. **Source release:** PR-ready non-`main` source, policy and `Release Check`,
   maintainer merge, post-merge local archive, and exact source SHA.
2. **Runtime safety and refresh:** approved B01 backup/restore readiness,
   R05 migration, R06 build/start, and local runtime provenance.
3. **Public validation:** separately approved stable named-Tunnel route, public
   exact provenance, and the five same-origin browser checks.

These are future deployment gates, not authorization to merge, refresh
production, change a Tunnel, run public smoke, use a live provider, or execute
Phase 119. The detailed runbooks remain subordinate procedures under these
three gates.

## Root causes and bounded disposition

The recalibration retained three release defects for repair: the receipt writer
fence ended between reservation and final publication; release children inherited
ambient `GIT_*` authority; and failed gates exposed only a suppressed-output
message. Stage 2 repairs each defect and adds a negative regression proof. PPL006,
telemetry, recovery redesign, workflow decommission, and other findings remain
residual and were not expanded into this stage.
